About SDG 14: Life Below Water

Sustainable Development Goal 14 (SDG 14) aims to conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources for sustainable development. Plastic pollution poses one of the most significant threats to achieving this goal, contaminating marine ecosystems from surface waters to the deep sea.

The Global Plastic Crisis: Statistics

  • 11 Million Tons: The amount of plastic waste that enters the oceans annually.
  • 5.2 Trillion: Estimated pieces of plastic in the ocean, with 4 billion plastic microfibers per square kilometer blanketing the deep sea.
  • 90% of Seabirds: Are estimated to have plastic fragments in their stomach.
  • 2050 Forecast: Plastic in the oceans could outweigh all fish unless action is taken.

Purpose: Why We Must Act

The purpose of the Aethera campaign aligns with SDG 14's targets to significantly reduce marine pollution of all kinds, in particular from land-based activities, including marine debris and nutrient pollution, by 2025. This requires collective awareness and systemic change.

"A single-use plastic item has an average usage time of 12 minutes, but it pollutes for hundreds of years."

Your Zero-Waste Toolkit

1. The 5 R's Philosophy

  • 1. Refuse: Say no to single-use plastics you don't need.
  • 2. Reduce: Minimize consumption overall.
  • 3. Reuse: Find new purposes for existing items.
  • 4. Repurpose: Creative reuse beyond the item's original function.
  • 5. Recycle: As a last resort, utilize local recycling programs.

2. DIY Sustainable Swaps

Practical alternatives to common plastic items:

  • Replace plastic wrap with reusable beeswax food wraps.
  • Use solid soap and shampoo bars instead of bottled products.
  • Carry a reusable water bottle and coffee cup everywhere.
  • Switch to bamboo toothbrushes and floss.
  • Make your own cleaning spray with vinegar and water.

3. Awareness & Tools

Key educational resources and apps:

  • Documentaries: "A Plastic Ocean," "Seaspiracy" (for awareness).
  • Cleanup Maps: Find local beach/park cleanup events in your area.
  • Policy Tracker: Check national and local legislation on plastic bans.
  • Refill App: Find nearby stations to refill water bottles.
  • Local Zero-Waste Stores: Support businesses committed to no-packaging.
